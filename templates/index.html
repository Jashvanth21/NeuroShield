Understanding Brain Tumors

What are Brain Tumors?

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain or surrounding tissues. They can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous) and can affect brain function depending on their location, size, and rate of growth.

Common Types of Brain Tumors:
  • Glioma: Develops from glial cells that support nerve cells. Includes astrocytomas, oligodendrogliomas, and ependymomas.
  • Meningioma: Forms in the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. Usually benign but can cause symptoms.
  • Pituitary Tumor: Affects the pituitary gland, which controls hormone production. Can cause hormonal imbalances.
  • Schwannoma: Develops from Schwann cells that protect nerve cells. Often affects the vestibular nerve.

Symptoms and Detection

Brain tumor symptoms vary depending on the tumor's location, size, and growth rate. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment.

Common Symptoms:
  • Headaches that worsen over time
  • Seizures or convulsions
  • Difficulty with balance or walking
  • Changes in vision, hearing, or speech
  • Personality or behavior changes
  • Nausea or vomiting
Detection Methods:

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is the most common and effective method for detecting brain tumors. Our AI-powered system analyzes MRI scans to identify tumors and provide detailed information about their type, size, and characteristics.
